Dogecoin and US Gas Prices

2021 has been an interesting year. While much of the American public looks forward to vaccinations and the re-entrance into society, our economic landscape is changing rapidly. The emergence of cryptocurrency over the last few years has had a lot of people wondering if they can get in on the action as well. Bitcoin's price has skyrocketed during most of the pandemic, and newly popularized Non-Fungible Tokens have begun popping up repeatedly. Dogecoin is an open source peer-to-peer digital currency, favored by Shiba Inus worldwide. While it began as a meme, Dogecoin has actually gone up in price by substantial amounts. The pandemic pushed most Americans into online jobs, and now we are seeing these NFTs everywhere. Additionally, the inital closures caused a huge decrease in the number of drivers on the road, and thus, gas prices dropped considerably. While these two are not directly related, it is interesting to see how this pandemic has affected the American public, from a change in the way we may view currency, to a change in how we get by without our cars.

Not many could have seen the rise of Dogecoin coming. According to the website, "Dogecoin sets itself apart from other digital currencies with an amazing, vibrant community made up of friendly folks just like you. "Doge" is our fun, friendly mascot! The Shiba Inu is a Japanese breed of dog that was popularized as an online meme and represents Dogecoin." The currency is light-hearted and fun, which is not usually how we typically think of currency. Typically, currency is thought of as a way to live, used to purchase consumption goods. While cryptocurrencies have changed the currency landscape as a whole, Dogecoin has done so in a unique fashion. The future of Dogecoin is uncertain, however, right now, it is simply okay to bask in the easy-going and fun nature of the Doge.

As we can see, Dogecoin sees a modest increase in price around early February, and it explodes in early April. While it appears to have started to decline over the past week or so, it is still worth several times as much as it was early in the year. I decided only to include the data from the Year to Date, since Dogecoin has barely stayed afloat since it began. There was a slight uptick at the beginning of 2018, but it has stayed just above $0.00 since then, until it really started to take off in latE January/early February. It will be interesting to see if this surge in price can continue, or if it is just a trend that will disappear soon.

Another place where we often saw a lot of variance was the early part of the COVID-19 pandemic, when lockdowns were underway, and cars began to disappear from roads. Around late April, 2020, gas prices dipped to their lowest level in about five years. As fewer drivers were being required to go into work, physically, the demand for gas simply fell off.

Obviously, this chart is a lot less sporadic and volatile than the Dogecoin prices, mainly due to the relative prices. One dogecoin is worth less than thirty cents, while a gallon of gas has fluctuated around $2.50 or so for the last several years. I decided to use a longer time period than that of Dogecoin, only to show the trend, and how it chagned during the early part of 2020. While the decrease in prices might not appear to be very high, it is certainly a substantial change of about $0.70 per gallon. In the case of gas prices, we have a pretty clear understanding of why this happened, compared to the Dogecoin trend. Overall, it can be hard to say what the future holds. Many investors are wary about jumping on board with new cryptocurrency trends, while others will regret it if they don't give it a shot. While Dogecoin may or may not be the future of crypto, it has certainly become an important part of how we understand NFTs, and will likely continue to play a part in its future.
