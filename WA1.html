Introduction¶

As we all know, COVID-19 has devestated the entire world, killing over 2 million people globally, including more than 400,000 in the United States (Our World In Data). In this analysis, we will look at how COVID has affected the United States. We will start with raw data from the Center for Disease Control (CDC) and eventually visualize how deadly the Coronavirus has been. In order to do this, we will need to create multiple dataframes, thereby isolating the variables of interest. There is a lot of false and/or misleading information when it comes to the Coronavirus, so we will attempt to present this data in an unbiased fashion. It is vital that people get the best possible information, so that they can keep themselves and others safe.

Conclusion¶

In this analysis, we have looked at how COVID-19 has impacted the United States. Looking at a specific region of interest, the Mountain West, we were able to see how Colorado compares to its neighbors, in terms of COVID-19 deaths as a proportion of total deaths. Although Colorado clearly had more total deaths from COVID-19, we saw that its proportion was quite similar in comparison to Wyoming, Utah, and New Mexico. We did this again for all states to see which states had the highest rates. Seeing New Jersey top that list was not surprising, however, the fact that nearly 1 out of every 5 deaths were attributable to Coronavirus is quite shocking. Being able to see just how much larger the second spike in deaths was makes it more clear about just how deadly this virus is. Ideally, we will not return to the high levels of death, but that will require help from everyone. Obviously, the pandemic has been catastrophic and will leave a lasting impact for years to come. So, it is vital that people get the best information possible, in order to make the best decisions to try and lessen the effect.
