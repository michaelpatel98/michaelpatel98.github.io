6. Deploy: Use your visualization with your target data to conduct the tasks you outlined in Step 3. Note your observations about the data gained through these tasks.

Cynthia Cooper holds the highest peak, in terms of Wins Generated, in the league's history. Lauren Jackson has an insanely high peak, in terms of PER (Player Efficiency Rating), and she dominated that category for a few years. Interestingly, these players all have similar USG_pct, or Usage Percentage, which implies that they are frequently facilitating the offense, something that makes sense for these elite-level players. Similarly, these players have very similar TS_pct, or Total Shooting Percentages. This is even more intriguing, since it isn't necessarily expected that they all shoot similar percentages. Total Shooting percentage incorporates both the 3-point field goal and free-throws, so for example, if a player only shoots 3-pointers and averages 20 points, this will be weighted as lower than a player who shoots only 2-pointers and averages the same 20. Considering this explanation, it makes it even more surprising that these players have Total Shooting percentages between, essentially, 50% and 60%, for their careers. Still, there are some major peaks and troughs, like Nneka Ogwumike's near 73.7% in 2016.

The target problem was to simply explore WNBA data. This was done by looking at both player and team data, which gave the ability to see, simply, who stands out. In the source article, the typical reader may certainly draw some meaningful conclusions, however, there is clear bias. Some may see the tables and recognize a few names that stand out, but the article's focus is on declaring Cynthia Cooper-Dyke the greatest of all time. While there is certainly some merit to that, she had a short stint in the league, despite dominating previously, overseas. The WNBA is only becoming more popular, and with that, new players will emerge to challenge the greats of the past. This has already begun, however, as we can see in the new visualization trends that I created. My solution allows the user to draw their own conclusions, which was the main target in the first place. After all, if a player truly is the greatest, it should probably be a general consensus among the public. Still, my solution is not perfect, since Cooper-Dyke's career came to a close at the relative beginning of the league, and so her true dominance is not displayed. Addressing this issue would require additional data, and likely some major data cleaning. Furthermore, I also looked at the team data, which was somewhat useful in targeting the problem as well. The main issue with the team data was a lack of depth and formatting. While this was an obstacle, data was still able to be collected and presented, giving a more general context of the league.
